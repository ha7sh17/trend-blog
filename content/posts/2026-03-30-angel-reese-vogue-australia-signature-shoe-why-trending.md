## The bigger business angle: why brands care

This trend is also a business signal.

For years, women athletes were often marketed in narrow lanes: performance campaigns, occasional lifestyle features, and limited signature-product storytelling. That model is changing quickly.

Reese’s moment suggests a newer playbook:

1. **Athlete as media entity** — direct narrative control through social channels.
2. **Athlete as design identity** — signature products featured in editorial contexts.
3. **Athlete as cultural bridge** — moving between sports, fashion, and entertainment without changing persona.

For brands, the upside is obvious:

- broader audience reach beyond core sports consumers,
- stronger emotional attachment to products,
- and faster conversion from attention to commerce.

For athletes, the upside is leverage: more ownership, more storytelling control, and more long-term brand equity.
