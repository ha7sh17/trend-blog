The Rockets, meanwhile, are proving they belong in the elite conversation. At 31-19, Houston is second in the Southwest Division and has established themselves as a legitimate threat in the West. A road win in Oklahoma City would send a powerful message to the rest of the conference.

The stakes couldn't be higher for both squads.

## Injury Report: Thunder Missing Their Superstar

One of the biggest storylines heading into this Rockets vs Thunder matchup is the lengthy injury list for Oklahoma City. The Thunder are without:

- **Shai Gilgeous-Alexander** (abdominal strain) — The MVP frontrunner is expected to be sidelined until after the All-Star break
- **Jalen Williams** (hamstring) — The All-NBA swingman is also on the shelf
- **Ajay Mitchell** — OKC's top bench ball handler
- **Nikola Topic and Thomas Sorber** — Both remain out recovering from injuries

However, there's good news for Thunder fans. **Chet Holmgren**, **Isaiah Hartenstein**, **Lu Dort**, and **Alex Caruso** all returned after sitting out Wednesday's loss to the Spurs for rest. That's a massive boost to OKC's frontcourt and defensive capabilities.

For Houston, the Rockets ruled out **Fred VanVleet**, **Steven Adams**, and **Jae'Sean Tate** (wrist contusion). **Amen Thompson** was initially listed as questionable with an illness but was upgraded to available before tip-off. **Tari Eason** also returned after missing Thursday's game for injury management.
