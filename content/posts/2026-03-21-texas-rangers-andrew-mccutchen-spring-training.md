## The key baseball question: what should Texas do with DH?

The Rangers’ challenge isn’t just choosing “best hitter in March.” It’s balancing:

- early-season matchup optimization,
- veteran contract realities,
- clubhouse role clarity,
- and medium-term depth insurance.

### The case for McCutchen getting immediate DH reps

- He’s currently winning the quality-of-contact and quality-of-plate-appearance conversation.
- His veteran strike-zone profile gives a floor against many right-handers.
- He can help Texas avoid extended dead spots if someone else opens cold.

### The case for patience with Pederson

- Pederson’s track record as a power lefty still carries value.
- The organization invested real money and likely still sees adjustment upside.
- Overreacting to spring can create April/May regret if underlying indicators normalize.

### The likely compromise

A rotating DH model early in the season is the most practical answer:

- use handedness and matchups,
- prioritize who is controlling the zone best week to week,
- keep both veterans in playable rhythm,
- and protect against a one-bat dependency.

That approach also gives Texas room to evaluate whether McCutchen’s spring gains persist under regular-season game planning.
