## The tournament storylines casual fans should know

If you’re just jumping in this weekend, these are the themes worth following.

### UConn’s undefeated pressure test

Going undefeated through a full season is difficult; finishing the job in a single-elimination tournament is historically brutal. One cold shooting half, one foul issue, one opponent heater — that’s all it takes.

That’s why every UConn game now carries two layers:

- immediate result (advance or not)
- historical consequence (legacy season or missed immortality)

### The power of the other 1-seeds

South Carolina, Texas, and UCLA aren’t supporting characters. They are legitimate title-level programs with deep rosters and tournament coaching experience.

The bracket argument isn’t just “Can UConn lose?” It’s also “Which elite peer is best built to meet UConn late?”

ESPN’s analysts pointed to teams like LSU and Louisville as potential disruptors. The reasons are familiar March formulas:

### Early-round games are no longer “filler”

One of the biggest shifts in the women’s game: first-weekend matchups now generate real mainstream anticipation. It’s not just fans waiting for the Final Four brands. There’s stronger appetite for underdog narratives, individual stars, and tactical chess matches from day one.

That shift is one of the healthiest indicators for the sport’s growth.
