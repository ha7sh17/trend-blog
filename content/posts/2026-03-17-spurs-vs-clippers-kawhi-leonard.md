## Tactical Matchup: What This Game Was Really About

Most fans see injuries and stop there. But the tactical matchup explains why analysts leaned Spurs.

### 1) Rebounding edge and extra possessions

One linked preview highlighted the Clippers’ weaker rebounding profile. Against a Spurs group that can generate second chances, that becomes a possession battle. In close playoff-style games, two or three extra possessions can decide everything.

### 2) Pace tension

The Clippers are generally more comfortable in controlled, slower possessions. The Spurs are at their best when they force tempo and attack early in the clock. If San Antonio pushes pace off misses and turnovers, they can drag L.A. into uncomfortable possessions.

### 3) Wembanyama’s rim impact

Even when he’s not scoring 35, Wembanyama bends entire offensive plans by taking away driving lanes and forcing floaters or kick-outs. Some betting/preview coverage also emphasized his recent block production and glass work. Whether or not you care about props, the underlying point is valid: his defensive event rate changes games fast.

### 4) Clippers shot creation without Kawhi

Without Leonard, the Clippers can still produce offense, but the hierarchy changes. More burden falls on secondary creators and new lineup combinations. That can work in stretches, but late-clock possessions become harder against length.

In short: this wasn’t just “Team A better than Team B.” It was “Team A has style leverage against Team B’s current roster state.”
