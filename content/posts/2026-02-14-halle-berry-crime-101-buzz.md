## *Crime 101* Background: Cast, Story, and the Michael Mann Comparisons

*Crime 101* is directed by Bart Layton, known for *American Animals*, and adapted from a Don Winslow novella. The story follows Mike (Chris Hemsworth), a methodical thief working California’s 101 corridor with strict rules, while LAPD detective Lou Lubesnick (Mark Ruffalo) tries to prove a pattern others miss.

Halle Berry’s Sharon isn’t just a side character. She functions as a pressure point in the system—someone with access, intelligence, and personal stakes. That role matters because thrillers like this rise or fall on credible intermediaries: people who can connect white-collar spaces with street-level risk.

A lot of reviewers mention Michael Mann influences, especially *Heat*, *Thief*, and *Collateral*-style atmosphere. The comparisons are less about copying plot beats and more about tone:
